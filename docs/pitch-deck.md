# DigitalBalance @home — Pitch Deck
**HIP 26 | Family Bonding Activity App**

---

## 1. Problem Definition

### The Problem

**Parents are losing presence with their children — and they know it.**

Smartphones have become the default gap-filler in family life. Parents who are aware of their screen overuse overwhelmingly want to change — but awareness alone does not work. Guilt-based apps, screen-time dashboards, and usage blockers produce anxiety without behavior change.

Research consistently shows that what *does* work is **structured, positive activity together** — not abstinence from screens, but replacing passive scroll time with intentional moments.

### Target Audience

| Segment | Profile |
|---|---|
| **Primary users** | Parents of children aged 5–10 in Germany |
| **Institutional gateway** | KITA and elementary school educators who coordinate parent groups |
| **System-level payer** | German statutory health insurers (Krankenkassen) |

**The pain is well-documented and universal** — it is not a niche concern. Every parent with a smartphone faces it; most feel guilt around it.

---

## 2. Solution

### DigitalBalance @home

A mobile app that turns offline family time into a tangible, rewarding experience — without ever framing the parent as the problem.

**How it works:**
1. Parents join a group (e.g., their child's school class or KITA cohort)
2. The group receives a **Challenge** — a curated set of age-appropriate, free activities to do with their child over a set period
3. As activities are completed, parents upload a photo — filling a shared **Collage**
4. The collage becomes a memory artifact: a visual record of family moments, not a performance tracker
5. No rankings. No deficits. No guilt. Only positive momentum.

**Key differentiators:**
- **Positive reinforcement by design** — the app never shows what you *haven't* done; it celebrates what you *have*
- **Socioeconomic accessibility** — all suggested activities are free or low-cost; paid content is never surfaced as primary
- **GDPR-native** — built for EU compliance from day one; no third-party analytics; photos never leave the parent's chosen group
- **School/KITA integration** — challenges can be distributed institution-wide, making adoption structural rather than individual

## 3. Market Analysis

### Market Size

| Segment | Estimate |
|---|---|
| Global parenting app market (2024) | ~€1.5B, growing at ~7% CAGR |
| Digital health & wellness apps (EU) | ~€8B by 2027 |
| Germany statutory health insurance (GKV) | ~73M insured members; §20 SGB V prevention budget |
| German children aged 5–10 | ~4.1M children → ~3.5M households |

**The §20 SGB V lever is central:** German Krankenkassen are legally required to fund certified prevention programs. A certified DigitalBalance @home is a reimbursable benefit — insurers bear the cost, families get it free.

### Scalability Path

```
Prototype (TUM / foundation cohort)
    ↓
Pilot with 3–5 KITA / elementary school groups
    ↓
Regional rollout via Stiftung Kindergesundheit network
    ↓
Insurance partnership → GKV-certified prevention program
    ↓
National / DACH expansion
```

The foundation's existing relationships with schools and insurers compress what would otherwise be a multi-year cold-outreach sales cycle into a warm, credentialed pilot.

## 4. Competition

### Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | What they do | Our differentiation |
|---|---|---|
| **Screen Time (Apple/Android built-in)** | Usage tracking + blocking | Negative framing, no positive replacement behavior |
| **Bark / Qustodio** | Parental control monitoring | Surveillance-oriented; increases family tension |
| **Headspace for Kids** | Mindfulness for children | Individual, not family-relational; no activity mechanic |
| **Hiku / Kinderzeit** | German screen-time tools | No community/group mechanic; no institutional channel |
| **Generic family organizer apps** | Scheduling, chores | No engagement loop; no photo-collage artifact |

**Our unique selling proposition:**
- The only app combining a **structured group challenge mechanic** with a **physical-world activity focus** and an **institutional distribution channel**
- The collage artifact creates emotional lock-in — families do not want to lose their memory album
- The KITA/school channel means adoption is driven by trusted community figures, not ads

### Intellectual Property

- No patents filed at this stage; the competitive moat is **distribution** (foundation network + insurer relationships) and **data** (a growing, curated activity pool matched to the German context)
- Codebase is proprietary; GDPR-compliant architecture is a barrier to fast followers in the EU market

## 5. Sustainable Business Model

### Revenue Model: B2B2C

**End users (parents) pay nothing.** Revenue comes from institutional and insurance partners.

| Channel | Model | Pricing (indicative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Health insurers (Krankenkassen)** | Per-active-user licensing fee; positioned as a §20 SGB V certified prevention benefit | €3–8 / member / month |
| **Schools & KITAs** | Annual institution license covering unlimited groups and challenges | €200–500 / institution / year |
| **Foundation / NGO grants** | Non-dilutive funding for prototype and pilot phases | Project-based |

### Go-to-Market Strategy

**Phase 1 — Prototype (now → 6 months)**
- Deploy with a small cohort via Stiftung Kindergesundheit contacts
- Validate engagement, collage completion rate, and parent NPS

**Phase 2 — Institutional Pilot (6–18 months)**
- Roll out to 5–10 KITA / elementary school groups in Munich area
- Gather outcome data (offline family time self-reported, parent wellbeing surveys) for insurer pitch

**Phase 3 — Insurance Partnership (18–36 months)**
- Submit for §20 SGB V certification with pilot outcome data
- First Krankenkasse partnership as a member benefit → primary revenue stream

**Why this works:**
- Stiftung Kindergesundheit provides credibility, trust, and warm introductions to both schools and insurers
- Insurers are actively seeking certified digital prevention programs with measurable outcomes
- The school channel ensures high family density per cohort — essential for the social group mechanic
